# Chart Selection Guide - My Notes

## The One Question: "What am I trying to SHOW?"

### 1. COUNT PLOT - "How many?"
**Use when:** [COunting involved]
**Example from my work:** [How many passenger died]

### 2. BAR PLOT - "Compare averages"
**Use when:** [avarage comparison]
**Example from my work:** [Average no. of female vs male passenger]

### 3. BOX/VIOLIN PLOT - "Show spread"
**Use when:** [age/ salaray range]
**Example from my work:** [Ticket Fare Distribution by Passenger Class]

### 4. LINE CHART - "Trend over time"
**Use when:** [timeline ]
**Example from my work:** [timeline for 30 days]

### 5. SCATTER PLOT - "Relationship"
**Use when:** [x related to y ]
**Example from my work:** [fare class higher survival rate higher]

## My Decision Process
When I look at data, I ask:

Is time involved? → LINE CHART
Am I counting "how many"? → COUNT PLOT
Am I comparing averages? → BAR PLOT
Am I showing spread/range? → BOX/VIOLIN PLOT
Am I checking if two numbers are related? → SCATTER PLOT


Example comparisons:
Question | Chart | Why
"How many people in each city?" Count Plot | No time, just categories
"How many visitors each day for 30 days?" Line Chart | Time series!
"How many males vs females?" Count Plot | No time, just counting
"Sales each month this year?" Line Chart | Time trend!

The trick: If categories have NATURAL ORDER (especially time), use LINE. If categories are just groups with no order, use COUNT.
