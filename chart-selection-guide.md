    "# When to Use Which Chart - The Simple Guide\n",
    "\n",
    "## 🎯 ASK YOURSELF ONE QUESTION: \"What am I trying to show?\"\n",
    "\n",
    "---\n",
    "\n",
    "## 1. COMPARING THINGS (Who's bigger? Which is best?)\n",
    "\n",
    "### BAR CHART - Comparing categories\n",
    "**Use when:** Comparing different groups/items\n",
    "**Examples:**\n",
    "- Which city has most population?\n",
    "- Top 5 selling products\n",
    "- Salary comparison between departments\n",
    "- Programming language popularity\n",
    "\n",
    "**Code:**\n",
    "```python\n",
    "plt.bar(categories, values)\n",
    "# or\n",
    "sns.barplot(data=df, x='category', y='value')\n",
    "```\n",
    "\n",
    "**Real question it answers:** \"Which one is bigger/smaller/best/worst?\"\n",
    "\n",
    "---\n",
    "\n",
    "## 2. SHOWING CHANGE OVER TIME (Trends, growth, decline)\n",
    "\n",
    "### LINE CHART - Trends over time\n",
    "**Use when:** Data changes across time periods\n",
    "**Examples:**\n",
    "- Stock price over months\n",
    "- Daily temperature for a week\n",
    "- Website visitors per day\n",
    "- Your weight loss journey over 3 months\n",
    "\n",
    "**Code:**\n",
    "```python\n",
    "plt.plot(time_periods, values)\n",
    "```\n",
    "\n",
    "**Real question it answers:** \"How did this change over time? Is it going up or down?\"\n",
    "\n",
    "---\n",
    "\n",
    "## 3. SHOWING PARTS OF A WHOLE (Percentages, proportions)\n",
    "\n",
    "### PIE CHART - Percentage breakdown\n",
    "**Use when:** Showing how 100% is divided\n",
    "**Examples:**\n",
    "- Budget breakdown (30% rent, 20% food, etc.)\n",
    "- Market share (Company A has 40%, B has 35%...)\n",
    "- Time spent on activities in a day\n",
    "- Student grades distribution (20% A, 30% B...)\n",
    "\n",
    "**Code:**\n",
    "```python\n",
    "plt.pie(values, labels=labels, autopct='%1.1f%%')\n",
    "```\n",
    "\n",
    "**Real question it answers:** \"What percentage does each part take?\"\n",
    "\n",
    "**WARNING:** Don't use if you have more than 6-7 categories (gets messy!)\n",
    "\n",
    "---\n",
    "\n",
    "## 4. SHOWING DISTRIBUTION (How data is spread)\n",
    "\n",
    "### HISTOGRAM - Frequency distribution\n",
    "**Use when:** Want to see how data is grouped/clustered\n",
    "**Examples:**\n",
    "- Age groups of employees (how many 20-30, 30-40, etc.)\n",
    "- Exam score distribution (how many scored 0-20, 20-40...)\n",
    "- Height distribution in a population\n",
    "- Income ranges in a city\n",
    "\n",
    "**Code:**\n",
    "```python\n",
    "plt.hist(data, bins=10)\n",
    "```\n",
    "\n",
    "**Real question it answers:** \"Where do most values fall? Are they clustered or spread out?\"\n",
    "\n",
    "---\n",
    "\n",
    "## 5. SHOWING RELATIONSHIP BETWEEN TWO THINGS\n",
    "\n",
    "### SCATTER PLOT - Correlation/relationship\n",
    "**Use when:** Checking if two things are related\n",
    "**Examples:**\n",
    "- Does more study hours = better grades?\n",
    "- Does age affect salary?\n",
    "- Relationship between height and weight\n",
    "- Ad spend vs sales revenue\n",
    "\n",
    "**Code:**\n",
    "```python\n",
    "plt.scatter(x_values, y_values)\n",
    "```\n",
    "\n",
    "**Real question it answers:** \"When X increases, does Y increase too? Are they related?\"\n",
    "\n",
